Adaptive Cross Approximation (ACA)

In the computational scienes low rank approximations play a key role. It is well known that the best rank-\(k\) approximation in terms of either the spectral norm of the Frobenius norm is the truncated Singular Value Decomposition (Eckart-Young-Mirsky Theorem). However the SVD is expensive to compute, as such it is of interest to find another ways of finding a good low rank approximation.

The simplest of these is the Adaptive Cross Approximation (ACA) which simple choses a set of row and column indexes and forms a low rank approximation that perfectly interpolates the chosen rows and columns. A visulization can be seen below

This picture is taken from [1]. A very solid introduction to Hiearchical Low-Rank Structures.

Adaptive Cross Approximation:

\[ G \approx G(:,c)(G(r,c))^{-1}G(r,:), \]

That the above expression perfectly interpolates the of rows (\(r\)) and columns (\(c\)) of G be seen by looking at the following matrix

\[ G = \begin{bmatrix} \overline{G} & \overline{G}(:,c)\\ \overline{G}(r,:) & G(r,c)\end{bmatrix} \]

Now compute the approximation

\[\begin{aligned} G(:,c)G(r,c)^{-1}G(r,:) &= \begin{bmatrix}\overline{G}(:,c)\\ G(r,c)\end{bmatrix}G(r,c)^{-1}\begin{bmatrix}\overline{G}(r,:)& G(r,c)\end{bmatrix}\\ &= \begin{bmatrix}\overline{G}(:,c)\\ G(r,c)\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}G(r,c)^{-1}\overline{G}(r,:)& I\end{bmatrix}\\ &= \begin{bmatrix}\overline{G}(:,c)G(r,c)^{-1}\overline{G}(r,:) & \overline{G}(:,c)\\ \overline{G}(r,:) & G(r,c)\end{bmatrix}, \end{aligned}\]

from which the perfect interpolations of rows \(r\) and columns \(c\) and can be seen.

How do we chose the index sets?

While the ACA approach is easily explained we still need a way to pick the best rows and columns. Because we want to be able to use the ACA on matrices of sizes where the SVD is not feasible we want a heuristic approach that scales close \(O(n)\).
