The Boundary Element Method

In this note we briefly explain how to discretize the following integral equation

\[ c(x)\phi(x) = \int_S \left[G(x,y)q(y) - F(x,y)\phi(y)\right]\ \mathrm{d}\mu \] \[ c(x)\phi(x) + \int_{S} F(x,y)\phi(y)\ \mathrm{d}\mu= \int_{S}G(x,y)q(y)\ \mathrm{d}\mu \]

Discretization

One can think of the domain boundary and discretization as (Photo from [1])
If we assume that the function is constant on each element we get the following

\[ \phi(y) = \phi_j, \quad q(y) = q_j, \quad y \in \Delta S_j \]

The idea is

\[ \frac{1}{2}\phi_i = \sum_{j=1}^N\int_{\Delta S_j}\left[G_iq_j - F_i\phi_j \right]\ \mathrm{d}S = \sum_{j=1}^N\left[\int_{\Delta S_j}G_i\ \mathrm{d}S\ q_j- \int_{\Delta S_j} F_i\ \mathrm{d}S\ \phi_j \right] \]

In order to easy the eyes we write the above as

\[ \frac{1}{2}\phi_i = \sum_{j=1}^N\left[g_{ij}q_j - \hat{f}_{ij}\phi_j \right], \quad i = 1,2,\dots, N \]

where

\[ g_{ij} = \int_{\Delta S_j} G_i\ \mathrm{d}S, \quad \hat{f}_{ij} = \int_{\Delta S_j} F_i\ \mathrm{d}S, \quad i,j = 1,2,\dots, N. \]

Now setting \(f_{ij} = \hat{f}_{ij} + \frac{1}{2}\delta_{ij}\) this can be rewritten into matrix form as follows

\[ \begin{bmatrix} f_{11} & f_{12} & \dots & f_{1N}\\ f_{21} & f_{22} & \dots & f_{2N}\\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots\\ f_{N1} & f_{N2} & \dots & f_{NN} \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} \phi_1 \\ \phi_2 \\ \vdots\\ \phi_N \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} g_{11} & g_{12} & \dots & g_{1N}\\ g_{21} & g_{22} & \dots & g_{2N}\\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots\\ g_{N1} & g_{N2} & \dots & g_{NN} \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} q_1 \\ q_2 \\ \vdots\\ q_N \end{Bmatrix} \]

Furthermore given that we on the boundary know the values of either \(\phi_i\) or \(q_i\) we can move every known value to the right for which we get the following system

\[ \begin{bmatrix} a_{11} & a_{12} & \dots & a_{1N}\\ a_{21} & a_{22} & \dots & a_{2N}\\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots\\ a_{N1} & a_{N2} & \dots & a_{NN} \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} \lambda_1 \\ \lambda_2 \\ \vdots\\ \lambda_N \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{Bmatrix} b_1 \\ b_2 \\ \vdots \\ b_N \end{Bmatrix}. \]

This off course requires some bookkeeping as we from this need to extract if \(\lamba_i\) is either a \(\phi\)-value or a \(q\)-value.
