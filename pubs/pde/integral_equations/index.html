Integral equations

\[ \int_a^b K(x,y)\phi(y)\ \mathrm{d}y = f(x),\\ \] \[ \phi(x) = \int_a^b K(x,y)\phi(y)\ \mathrm{d}y + g(x), \]

in which \(\phi\) is an unknown function, \(K(x,y)\) is a known kernel function, and \(f\) and \(g\) are two given functions. What we see in (1) is a linear Fredholm equation of the first kind wheres (2) is a linear Fredholm equation of the second kind. The kernel function \(K(x,y)\) determines the characteristics of the intergral equation. For example, if:

\[ K(x,y) = \frac{1}{|x - y|}, \]

then the integrals in (1) and (2) are singular when \(x\in [a,b]\).
