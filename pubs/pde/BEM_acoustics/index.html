The Boundary Element Method (BEM)

[1] \[ \Delta \phi + k^2\phi + Q\delta(x,x_Q) = 0, \quad \forall x\in E \]

  1. Sound pressure is given:

\[ \phi = \overline{\phi}, \quad \forall x \in S \]

  1. Particle velocity is given:

\[ q \equiv \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial n} = \overline{q} = i\omega \rho v_n, \quad \forall x \in S \]

  1. Impedance of the surface is given:

\[ \phi = Z v_n, \quad \forall x\in S \]

Sommerfeld radiation condition:

\[ \lim_{R\rightarrow \infty} \left[R \left|\frac{\partial \phi}{\partial R} - ik\phi\right| \right] = 0 \] \[ c(x) \phi(x) = \int_S \left[G(x,y,\omega)q(y) - F(x,y,\omega)\phi(y) \right] \mathrm{d}S_y + \phi^I(x) + QG(x,x_Q, \omega) \] \[ c(x) = \begin{cases} 1, \quad & \forall x \in E\\ 1/2, \quad & \forall x \in S \text{(smooth around x)}\\ 0, \quad & \forall x \notin E \cup S \end{cases} \] \[\begin{aligned} G(x,y,\omega) &= \frac{1}{4\pi r}e^{ikr}, F(x,y,\omega) &\equiv \frac{\partial G(x,y,\omega)}{\partial n(y)} = \frac{1}{4\pi r^2}(ikr - 1)r_{,j}n_j(y)e^{ikr}, \end{aligned}\]
